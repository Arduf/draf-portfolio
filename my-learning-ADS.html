Structuring Ads Campaign

Improved targeting for the right audiences. Ad structuring is an important factor to consider. A clear and organized ad structure makes it easier to scale campaigns. We can separate segments for each type of target and control the performance of each campaign properly. Moreover, structured ads make easier analysis and reporting of which ads, keywords, or ad groups are performing well and are data-driven.

Keyword Research

Keyword research can identify what potential customers are increasing engagement. The right keywords can help ads relevant to search queries of the target audience. This relevance increases the user experience. It can also help to avoid overspending when you have competitors.

Bidding Strategies

Bidding strategies help us control how much each click or impression costs. Different bidding can achieve each goal, such as maximizing clicks in a jumpstart campaign or a conversion rate after the campaign has insight data. A good bidding strategy can help you get better ad positions and effectiveness without necessarily increasing costs.

Enhance Ads Performance
  • Negative keywords: filter out irrelevant traffic to avoid wasted time.
  • Call-to-Action and Ad Extensions: To improve compelling ads and make them interactive with our ads.
  • Remarketing: target users who have previously visited our ad or website.
  • Landing Page: Mobile-friendliness and relevant content are important ways to increase UX for audiences.
  • Bidding: After receiving the data insight, calculate the ROAS, ROI, or conversion rate. It is important to adjust the bidding of each ad group or campaign to make sure our ad doesn't overspend and is doing the best it can.

A/B Testing

A/B testing is crucial for determining which ad group performs better in terms of conversions or impressions, depending on the test's objective. To begin, I launch a jumpstart ad to gather initial data insights. Then, I analyze this data, focusing on elements like keywords, ad copy, CPC, and bidding strategies. The insights gained from A/B testing serve as a valuable guide for future campaigns, allowing me to fine-tune my approach. This process helps me tailor my campaigns to reach my target audience more effectively.

Conversion Tracking

Conversion data is crucial for various reasons, including remarketing, product improvement, and tailoring promotions. I've set up Google Analytics on my website and linked it to my Google Ads account to track ad clicks. Additionally, I've created a conversion ad group to monitor users who submit their contact information for promotions. This allows me to collect emails for remarketing to cart abandoners using the MailPoet plugin in WordPress. By focusing on these conversion metrics, I can optimize my marketing strategy and improve customer engagement.

The conversion value is very important data that I can calculate with the cost of ads to determine the actual profitability of advertising efforts. In a similar way is the calculation of ROAS because the conversion value is the parameter that I can use to calculate the ROAS.

Bidding Strategies (Maximized CPC)

The insights from conversion rate data are crucial for optimizing Cost Per Click (CPC). This data helps me calculate the maximum CPC for our ad group, which determines the actual cost paid to Google Ads. Google Ads offers various bidding options like manual CPC, Enhanced CPC (ECPC), and Cost Per Acquisition (CPA), each suited to different campaign objectives. Choosing the right bidding strategy is key to achieving targets without overspending. Additionally, Max CPC influences ad rank, affecting where ads appear on search result pages. It's important to note that the landing page quality score is a significant factor in this calculation, making it a critical element to consider in the overall advertising strategy.
